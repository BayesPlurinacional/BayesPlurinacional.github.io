Code of Conduct

The first right is to be part of a community.

Codes of conduct are often similar to Article 12 of the Colombian National Constitution:

"No one shall be subjected to enforced disappearance, torture, or cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment or punishment."
When the indigenous Wayúu people translated this article, they wrote: "No one may place themselves above another’s heart or harm another person, even if they think and speak differently."

The Bayes Plurinacional event aims to foster an environment of respect, collaboration, and peaceful coexistence. In our communities, conflicts are resolved through gestures, exchanges, and mutual assistance.

Principles of Good Living (Aymara)

  • Suma Manq'aña: Knowing how to eat; we are what we eat.
  • Suma Umaña: Knowing how to drink; water is the source of all life.
  • Suma Thokoña: Knowing how to dance; act with spiritual awareness.
  • Suma Ikiña: Knowing how to sleep; rest to restore energy.
  • Suma Irnakaña: Knowing how to work; communal work is a joy.
  • Suma Lupiña: Knowing how to meditate; personal silence restores harmony.
  • Suma Amuyaña: Knowing how to think; follow the path of the heart.
  • Suma Munaña Munayasiña: Knowing how to love and be loved.
  • Suma Ist'aña: Knowing how to listen; perceive with the whole body.
  • Suma Aruskipaña: Knowing how to speak; words leave marks on hearts.
  • Suma Samkasiña: Knowing how to dream; dreams are the beginning of reality.
  • Suma Sarnaqaña: Knowing how to walk; no one walks alone.
  • Suma Churaña, Suma Katukaña: Knowing how to give and receive with gratitude.
