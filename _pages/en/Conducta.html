Code of Conduct

The first right is to be part of a community.

Codes of conduct often resemble Article 12 of the Colombian National Constitution: "No one shall be subjected to forced disappearance, torture, or cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment or punishment." When the Wayuu people, who endured 500 years of colonialism, translated this article, they wrote: "No one may cast aside another's heart or harm another, even if they think or say otherwise."

Bayes Plurinacional is a festive rite designed to create and renew our bonds. In community systems, conflicts are resolved through gestures, exchanges, and mutual aid.

As the last principle of Buen Vivir (Aymara version) states: Suma Churaña, Suma Katukaña. We must learn to give and receive with joy and gratitude.

Principles of Buen Vivir (Aymara)

  • Suma Manq'aña: Knowing how to eat; we are what we eat.
  • Suma Umaña: Knowing how to drink; water is the source of life.
  • Suma Thokoña: Knowing how to dance; act with spiritual dimension.
  • Suma Ikiña: Knowing how to sleep; rest to renew energy.
  • Suma Irnakaña: Knowing how to work; community work brings joy.
  • Suma Lupiña: Knowing how to meditate; personal silence restores the environment.
  • Suma Amuyaña: Knowing how to think; walk the path of the heart.
  • Suma Munaña Munayasiña: Knowing how to love and be loved.
  • Suma Ist'aña: Knowing how to listen; perceive with your whole body.
  • Suma Aruskipaña: Knowing how to speak; words leave a mark on hearts.
  • Suma Samkasiña: Knowing how to dream; dreams are the beginning of reality.
  • Suma Sarnaqaña: Knowing how to walk; no one walks alone.
  • Suma Churaña, Suma Katukaña: Knowing how to give and receive with gratitude.
