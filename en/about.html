Towards the Bayes Plurinational School-Congress 2024

The Plurinational Bayes community successfully held the Plurinational Bayesian Congress 2023 on August 4, 5, and 6 at the facilities of the Secretariat of Science and Technology of the province of Santiago del Estero, Argentina. The disciplinary and regional diversity was extraordinary. During the congress it became clear that there was an urgent need for more instances of theoretical and practical training in Bayesian methods, giving rise to the creation of the Plurinational Bayes School-Congress 2024.
The Plurinational Bayes School aims to enhance the training opportunities in research and/or application of Bayesian inference by offering workshops conducted by specialists from our region. Additionally, it seeks to foster connections, skills, and interaction to facilitate learning and the practical implementation of these concepts. Therefore, on August 6, 2024, the Plurinational Bayes School-Congress will begin in Salta, Argentina. For 3 days, we will gather at the National University of Salta to conduct practical workshops, presentations, debate tables, and poster sessions, concluding with a Hackathon. Register for the Plurinational Bayes School-Congress 2024 here. You have time until April 30. To propose virtual events, you can permanently access the following form.

Are you interested in giving a workshop, a talk, a debate, a poster? Please, complete the following form. Proposals will be accepted until April 30, 2024. For further information, please send an e-mail to bayesplurinacional@gmail.com
  • Initial level workshops "Empirical truths" (9 am - 12 pm): Broad, comprehensive, and in-depth view on: Bayesian networks; causal inference and model evaluation; information theory and data operationalization; time series and approximations; decision-making and betting.

  • Intermediate level workshops "Optimal beliefs" (1 pm - 4 pm): Workshops of 2 to 3 hours duration on concrete applications currently used in the public sector, private sector, or academia. They will be taught by individuals who currently employ Bayesian methods in their work, and it is expected that they will teach to replicate part of the inference process commonly used for solving real problems.

  • Presentations and debates "Intersubjective agreements" (5 pm - 7 pm): It will be organized in tables with presentations of 15 minutes approximately, in charge of referents of diverse disciplines that work with Bayesian methods. There will also be poster presentations and panel discussions on topics of socio-environmental interest.

  • Hackathon "Life bets": It will be an inference problem with bets and resource exchange that requires knowledge of time series and decision making, which will be covered in the initial workshop.
