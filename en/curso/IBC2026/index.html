Causal Bayesian Inference 1 is offered as an elective in the undergraduate and graduate programs of the School of Science and Technology at Universidad Nacional de San Martín (UNSAM). It is also open to the Bayes Plurinacional community.

Introduction and goals

All data-driven sciences develop causal arguments to explain and predict the world. The evaluation of causal hypotheses is attracting increasing interest from industry, which needs to measure the real impact of its actions. The advantage of causal models lies in their predictive capacity, which naturally adapts to contextual changes such as human interventions.

In this course we will review the foundations of evaluating alternative causal models P(M|D), methods for making causal predictions P(H|D,M), and optimal decision-making as an inference problem. Against the vertigo of an AI landscape filled with ephemeral tools, this course prioritizes immutable foundations.

Units

  2. Unit 2. Inference methods and probabilistic programming
  3. Unit 3. Causal predictions
  4. Unit 4. The algorithm zoo

  7. Unit 7. Decision-making

Instructor


Gustavo Landfried

Gustavo Landfried is a professor of Causal Bayesian Inference at the School of Science and Technology of Universidad Nacional de San Martín. After obtaining a degree in Social Anthropology, he completed his PhD in Computer Science at the Universidad de Buenos Aires under the supervision of the Applied Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (Diego Slezak) and the Interdisciplinary High-Performance Computing Laboratory (Esteban Mocskos).

There he specialized in skill analysis for the video game industry using Bayesian methods, developing and maintaining state-of-the-art packages in Python, Julia and R (TrueSkill Through Time). He currently works in industry (Muttdata) as a Data Scientist, developing Bayesian and causal inference projects for major transnational companies in Latin America. He is also co-founder and coordinator of the bayesplurinacional.org community.
